Iceland

Iceland

Mar 7, 2022 Country Overview 2 min read 9/10

Overview

A fantastic country that left us absolutely spellbound during our one-week trip, we started planning a return visit before the trip was even over. It absolutely does not belong to this world. Photos and videos cannot do it justice. One of those places every human being must see before they die.

We went in March hoping to catch the Northern Lights, but the weather was too overcast during our week so we missed them. The harsh weather conditions also meant we couldn't fully enjoy the natural sites. The only upside of going in winter was experiencing the brutal conditions firsthand, we saw Iceland like a true Icelander. Beyond that, to fully enjoy the natural beauty, going in summer makes much more sense. For Northern Lights specifically, the weather here is too unpredictable, trying Norway or similar destinations would be smarter.

If you can manage it, the ideal plan is a summer trip of at least 2 weeks, doing a full loop around the island.

What to See

  • Kerid Crater: A crater lake. We saw it frozen in winter, absolutely mesmerizing.
  • Gljufrabui: A waterfall you can actually walk inside. Bring a full-body raincoat and spare shoes and t-shirt.
  • Skogafoss: Climbing to the top is absolutely worth it, phenomenal panorama.
  • Diamond Beach: Words fail. Utterly magnificent. Set aside at least 2-3 hours to fully soak it in.
  • Svinafellsjokull Glacier: We joined a half-day glacier climbing tour and it was the most impactful thing we did in Iceland. Next time we're doing the full-day tours. Can be combined with ice cave visits.
  • Dyrholaey Lighthouse: The scenery and atmosphere here are also magnificent. Worth a stop.
  • Solheimajokull: Incredibly windy in winter, but summer conditions might be milder. The kind of place you could sit and watch for hours.
  • Reynisfjara Beach: I thought photos overhyped it, but seeing it in person was absolutely enchanting. Incredibly mystical atmosphere. Watch out for the waves though.
  • Icelandic Lava Show: We expected a cheesy tourist trap and went in with low expectations, turned out to be a highly informative, genuinely scientific experience. Seeing real lava is an incredible experience.
  • Hallgrimskirkja: Absolutely one of the most impressive cathedrals I've ever seen. Simple, understated, yet utterly captivating.

Food & Drink

  • Le Kock: A solid burger joint in central Reykjavik.
  • Forrettabarinn: Everything we ate was excellent, a great dining spot in Reykjavik.
  • 101 Bistro: Their fish and chips were superb. The owner is Turkish, say hello if you stop by.
  • Fosshotel Glacier Lagoon: We ate at this hotel while exploring the southern part. Again, everything was great.

Tips & Advice

Especially if going in winter, bring plenty of spares for everything. You will get wet on many days, and walking around in wet shoes and socks in freezing cold is not something you want.

A fisherman's raincoat we bought from Decathlon for 60 lira, which we almost didn't buy because it looked a bit cheap, literally saved our lives. While many people's much more expensive Iceland-purchased raincoats were ripping apart, ours held up great and kept us warm. Usable even in summer for waterfalls and sudden rain.
