Budapest

Aug 9, 2023 City Guide 1 min read 8/10

Overview

A city we revisited during the 2023 Sziget Festival. Since we'd already covered most of the tourist sights on our first visit, this time we could explore without the pressure of missing anything, which made it much more enjoyable. We also got to properly enjoy the festival. If you're coming for Sziget and it's also your first time in Budapest, I'd recommend adding at least 1-2 extra nights beyond your plan to avoid cramming sightseeing.

Food & Drink

If you're into wine tasting, this city is absolutely your kind of place. There were more wine bars here than we'd encountered even in Paris. Great flavors, very reasonable prices. My top three recommendations: Champion Wine Vinarium & Gastro Boutique, Tasting Table Budapest Shop, and Tokaj Wine Bar. The first one was especially phenomenal, incredible quality wines for the price we paid. Whatever you do, don't leave without trying a proper Tokaji Aszu.

Nightlife

For nightlife, Szimpla Kert and Instant are the most popular spots. We walked into Szimpla Kert casually around 7 PM. Left to eat dinner since they don't serve food, came back around 9 and waited 15-20 minutes to get in. By the time we left at 10:30-11, they weren't letting anyone in and the queue was 3-4 times longer than what we'd waited in. Bottom line: don't leave your visit too late. Same story at Instant, we walked right in at 11 PM when it was partially empty, but by midnight it was packed and by 1 AM the queue outside was matching Szimpla's. Going a bit earlier lets you enjoy both far more.

Where to Stay

We stayed at the Ikonik Parlament Hotel and were happy with everything. Recommended.
