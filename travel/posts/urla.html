Urla

Aug 21, 2023 Food Wine 1 min read 9/10

Overview

Urla is a vacation town that has become increasingly known for its gastronomy. After taking visiting friends to the restaurants here, I consistently hear feedback that these chef restaurants put even the Michelin-starred places in Istanbul to shame. Prices are a bit inflated, sure, but then again, where in Turkey isn't like that right now? At least you get to enjoy beautiful, distinctive food, and that counts for something.

Food & Drink

Beyond the chef restaurants, the seafood places are genuinely top-quality, serving superb seafood meze and hot appetizers. Our favorite chef restaurant is Vino de Locale, and for seafood, Akin'in Yeri.

The vineyard route is truly delightful. I can confidently say there isn't a single winery with bad wine. One or two might be average, but the rest are all seriously impressive. Our favorites for wine quality are MMG, USCA, and Perdix. For ambiance, it's definitely Urla Sarapcilik.

What to See

Don't set high expectations for the sea. The Cesmealti-Iskele side is generally seaweedy, and the Demirciler side is freezing cold. If you live in the area, it's better than nothing, but coming from another city specifically to swim in Urla isn't really worth it. You're better off heading to Mordogan (Manal) or Cesme (Ilica). The August winds make Cesmealti beach quite suitable for windsurfing, though.

Tips & Advice

If you come as a day-tripper and just sit by the sea all day without even staying for dinner, you'll probably leave unimpressed. But if you stop by a couple of wineries and cap the evening off with a great restaurant dinner, you'll have a fantastic time. I think that's why most people who praise Urla do so, it's the food-and-wine combination that makes it special.

Wineries

In its current state, it's back to its former performance.

ÇAKIR Winery & Vineyards/ ÇAKIR Şarapçılık & BağcılıkSep 2025★★★★☆

2022: The place has a nice ambiance, we didn't eat but did a wine tasting. The presentation and service were good. Their Chardonnay wines are truly incredible. TERUAR URLA2025★★★★☆

A restaurant that doesn't give you an a la carte option if you go with more than 5 people, only the tasting menu. I respect that decision, but it was a bit disappointing that they didn't mention it during the reservation. Food quality is generally good. There are different plates. Some were skippable while others were really great. The okra vellutata was truly amazing, a signature dish. We also really liked the Cretan zucchini. The desserts at the end were standard though. The tasting menu is filling overall. But there's way too much downtime between courses. We sat down at 8 and didn't finish all the plates until 11:30. The wine menu is quite extensive, and the sommelier was really attentive. The staff is very friendly and fun. You can tell they're happy with their working conditions. The bathroom doors open inward. That doesn't meet safety standards for an emergency. Whether this place is worth the money at today's prices is debatable, but I guess that could be said about most places given the current economic situation.

Statera Şarapçılık | Statera Winery2025★★★★★

The newest winery in the area. Some of their wines are good, some still have a way to go, but they'll get much better down the road. They've got potential. The staff is very attentive, and the tasting setting is nice. Definitely a must-visit.

HUS Şarapçılık2023★★★★☆

The wines are great value for money, we especially loved the emir wine. The crispy parmesan they serve on the side is really well done :)

Perdix Şarapçılık2023★★★★★

We went on a beautiful Sunday. I can't remember the last time I saw a crowd like that :) definitely worth going on calmer days, weekdays if possible. Getting in and out of the parking lot was really tough with the crowd. There were no seats at the tables but we'd come for a tasting anyway and that was available. The person doing the tasting was very attentive, bless them, gave us plenty of information. Wine quality is generally good, prices might be a touch high; I'd say these are wines that target the premium segment rather than value-for-money. Absolutely worth going, seeing, and tasting. Especially the bogazkere 2018 and cabernet sauvignon 2019 were really impressive. Update after our second visit: This time we got to eat as well. The pappardelle was truly excellent, we had two plates. The barrel-aged cabernet sauvignon 2019 was genuinely an incredible flavor.

Urlice Vineyards★★★☆☆

The wines are average (could be specific to the vintage we tasted, might get better with a bit more aging) but they especially need to work on communication and service for the restaurant side. At the tasting session we went to with a reservation, we waited about 15 minutes because other guests were late. During this wait, nobody came to inform us (we went and asked ourselves twice), nor did anyone come to ask if we needed anything. (It's really strange for a customer to sit at a table for nearly 20-25 minutes without a waiter even bringing a menu) On a previous visit, we'd ordered 4 different glasses of wine thinking let's try as many different wines as possible; when we asked for extra empty glasses so we could share with our friends, they told us they don't offer that kind of service and that's not how they operate. A bit of a strange establishment :) unfortunately there's a bit of smugness and pretentiousness going on. 3 stars for the beauty of the setting and especially in honor of the effort put into winemaking & the lady who gave us detailed information about the wines during the tasting.

Mozaik Şarapçılık / Mozaik Winery2022★★★★★

I'm not sure there's another place that offers wines this aged at such reasonable prices. All their products are definitely very high quality. I didn't feel a standout where I'd say 'this one is incredible' but the Marselan stood out a bit more on the nose, and the Ekigania was good on the palate. The venue itself is very simple. There's a small building for tastings and no food service at all. It's worth going with the awareness that you're just there for a tasting. The horses look beautiful and the grounds are very spacious, makes you wish they'd set up a system where regular people could ride too.

MMG winery2022★★★★★

Their white wines in particular are excellent, and the venue's view is stunning. The owners are very friendly and helpful. A true value-for-money spot. We were really impressed by the Bornova Muscat and the Viognier-Chardonnay blend. We'll definitely visit again when their new vintages come out.

Ayda Bağları Winery & Vineyards2022★★★☆☆

We visited for a wine tasting. Unfortunately in terms of value for money, they fall well short compared to other wineries in the area. Prices are high, and the wine they poured into tasting glasses was quite little. We weren't impressed at an 'oh this is really good' level by any of their wines. The venue itself is definitely nice, maybe the food or accommodation are good (we didn't try those) but we wouldn't recommend going just for a wine tasting.

Urla Sarapcilik★★★★☆

The venue is absolutely perfect, you feel like you're in Hollywood. The wines, especially Tempus, are very good, but if you start comparing value for money, you can find much higher quality wines on the market. Definitely worth going for the venue, and the tasting fees are reasonable and worth joining; but think twice before buying bottles I'd say :)
