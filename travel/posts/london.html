London

Jan 22, 2024 City Guide 1 min read 8/10

Overview

We visited London when the forecast showed 7-12 degrees. Turkey was actually showing colder temperatures at the time (3-10), so we were thrilled thinking we were heading somewhere warmer. We absolutely froze. The wind is brutal. It cuts right through you. Don't be fooled by the temperature readings; dress properly warm no matter what.

Tips & Advice

Since we were there for education and it was our second visit, we explored at a pretty relaxed pace. London might actually be the perfect city for that kind of trip. We got lucky with no rain during our stay, so aside from the wind, nothing spoiled our sightseeing.

The city is massive but the metro system works brilliantly. You can get to all tourist areas quickly and safely. It's a true metropolis, though a bit poorly planned.

After work hours, everywhere is packed. Finding a restaurant or bar between 5 and 7 PM is really tough. Without a reservation, the odds of good places being taken are very high. We recommend making reservations even for bars.

What to See

We couldn't skip Camden Town again, one of our favorite areas in the city. It was much calmer on our previous visit, but this time we went on a Saturday afternoon and it was incredibly crowded. We probably waited 10-20 minutes just at the metro entrance and exit, and couldn't find a single empty spot. If possible, visit on a weekday. And if you go, don't miss Cyberdog, an absolute legendary venue.
